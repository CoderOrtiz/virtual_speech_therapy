Get to know your Speech Therapists...

Emily Herndon, MA, CCC-SLP

Emily Herndon, MA, CCC-SLP is a certified Speech-Language Pathologist and Sole Owner of Speak Up. She has experience in evaluating and treating a wide range of communication and swallowing disorders in the adult population. She has a special interest in working with individuals with Parkinson's disease to regain and sectiontain clear, strong speech. Emily is SPEAK OUT! certified through the Parkinson's Voice Project. Please see below for more details on Emily's clinical experience and certifications as well as her journey in starting Speak Up.

​Tawni Palotta, MA, CCC-SLP is a certified Speech-Language Pathologist who received both her undergraduate and graduate training at the University of Florida. She has several years’ experience working in various settings including skilled nursing facilities, pediatric outpatient clinics, and acute rehabilitation. ​Tawni has worked in a virtual setting evaluating/treating pediatric clients for a wide range of speech, language, and pragmatic communication disorders. She strives to continuously integrate creative methods and evolving evidence-based practice techniques into her therapy for optimal outcomes.

Kami Kubler Lizik, MS, CCC-SLP

Kami Kubler Lizik, MS, CCC-SLP is a certified Speech-Language Pathologist who received her undergraduate education at the University of Pittsburgh and her masters training at James Madison University. She has several years’ experience working in a variety of settings including skilled nursing facilities, inpatient rehabilitation, and most recently, pediatric outpatient and early intervention services via a teletherapy platform. Kami's clinical expertise includes aphasia, cognitive-communication skills, and bilingual (Spanish) speech and language development and disorders. Kami loves working with clients of all ages to improve overall communication and quality of life.

Our Vision

Our vision is to provide quality, convenient speech therapy to clients from the comfort of their own home, while also raising awareness and support for a variety of global & local humanitarian organizations. This is accomplished by donating all monthly Speak Up profits to these organizations. Recipient organizations are selected each month by Speak Up clients & their families. Clients and family members can "speak up for the poor & helpless" by casting their vote via the "VOTE" tab in the menu above. Anyone is welcome to donate directly through links to organizations' websites provided on the "DONATE" page.

More about Emily...

Emily received both her undergraduate and graduate degrees from the University of Florida in Gainesville. She completed her clinical fellowship training at the Malcom-Randall VA Medical Center in Gainesville, Florida. After her fellowship she was hired on as full-time staff and continued to grow her clinical skills there from 2019-2021. In 2021, Emily decided it was time to pursue her career dream by starting her own private practice with a mission-minded vision. Thus, Speak Up Virtual Speech Therapy LLC was born! While building up her Speak Up clientele she works part-time at a number of skilled nursing and adult outpatient settings.

Emily has years of experience in evaluating and treating individuals with communication and swallowing disorders. She also had the opportunity of providing virtual speech therapy services via the VA's telehealth platform during her time at the VA. With this experience, Emily is equipped to provide quality and convenient virtual speech therapy services. Of course, not everyone's needs will be able to be met entirely through virtual services and in those circumstances, Emily will ensure her clients receive appropriate referrals to providers offering in-person services.

Emily's expertise includes delivering speech, language, voice, cognitive, and swallowing teletherapy to individuals with a wide variety of etiologies including stroke, movement disorders, head and neck cancer, traumatic brain injury, other neurogenic etiologies, cardiopulmonary disease, and a variety of voice disorders such as presbyphonia (aging voice), muscle tension dysphonia, vocal cord paralysis, etc.

As mentioned previously, Emily has a passion for working with individuals with Parkinson's Disease to improve and maintain their speaking abilities. Emily is a certified SPEAK OUT! provider through the Parkinson's Voice Project. See more information about the SPEAK OUT! Program at https://www.parkinsonvoiceproject.org/. In the near future, Emily would also like to start a therapy/support group for individuals with Parkinson's Disease who are interested in maintaining their speaking abilities following individual therapy and developing a community of support amongst other individuals with Parkinson's.
